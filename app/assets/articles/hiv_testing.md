# Understanding HIV Testing Intervals

---

## Key Points

- Everyone between the ages of **13 and 64** should get tested for HIV at least once.
- People with certain risk factors should get tested more often.
- Most HIV tests are available for free or at a reduced cost.
- Visit **https://gettested.cdc.gov** to find HIV testing in your area.

---

## Why Get Tested

The only way to know your HIV status is to get tested. Knowing your HIV status gives you powerful information to keep you and your partner healthy.

- If your test result is **positive**, you can begin treatment to live a long, healthy life and protect others.
- If your test result is **negative**, you can take steps to prevent HIV.

---

## When to Get Tested

Everyone between ages **13–64** should get tested at least once.

You should get tested **at least once a year** if:

- You are a man who has had sex with another man.
- You have had anal or vaginal sex with someone who has HIV.
- You have had more than one sex partner since your last HIV test.
- You have shared needles or injection equipment.
- You have exchanged sex for money or drugs.
- You have been diagnosed with another STI, hepatitis, or tuberculosis.
- You have had sex with someone whose HIV status you do not know.

### Gay and Bisexual Men
Sexually active gay and bisexual men may benefit from testing every **3–6 months**.

### Pregnant Individuals
Pregnant people should be tested during each pregnancy to prevent transmission to babies.

---

## Types of HIV Tests

### 1. Antibody Test
Detects antibodies to HIV in blood or oral fluid.  
Window period: **23–90 days**

### 2. Antigen/Antibody Test
Detects both antibodies and the p24 antigen.  
Window period: **18–45 days (lab test)**

### 3. Nucleic Acid Test (NAT)
Detects the virus itself in blood.  
Window period: **10–33 days**

---

## Window Period

The window period is the time between HIV exposure and when a test can detect infection.

| Test Type | Detection Time |
|-----------|---------------|
| Antibody Test | 23–90 days |
| Rapid Antigen/Antibody | 18–90 days |
| Lab Antigen/Antibody | 18–45 days |
| NAT | 10–33 days |

If you test negative after possible exposure, test again after the window period.

---

## After You Get Results

### If Positive
- A follow-up test will confirm the result.
- Begin HIV treatment immediately.
- Treatment can reduce viral load to undetectable levels.
- Undetectable = effectively no sexual transmission risk.

### If Negative
- Retest if within the window period.
- Continue preventive measures.
- Test annually if at ongoing risk.
