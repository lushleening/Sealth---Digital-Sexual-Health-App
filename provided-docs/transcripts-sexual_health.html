COMP2300 Transcripts 25/26: Sexual Health Services

These transcripts are provided for educational use only, and should not be used as research data.

The following eight transcripts record interviews with participants who have engaged with sexual health services. They have been purposively sampled to include a diverse range of users with different backgrounds and needs.

P1

Gender:Man
Age:22
Location:Manchester

Interviewer Good morning. Please could you tell me a little bit about how you have used sexual health services in the past?

Participant Morning. OK. I've used sexual health services a few times over the past couple of years. The first time I went was when I had some irritation and I was a bit panicked, to be honest. I didn’t really know what to expect, but I booked an appointment at a clinic near where I live in Manchester. Since then, I’ve been a couple more times, usually when I felt like something wasn’t quite right. I know you can go for regular check-ups, but I’ve only gone when there’s been a specific reason, like symptoms or when I’ve been in a new relationship and wanted to be sure everything was okay.

Interviewer Was the potential of more regular check-ups discussed with you when you visited?

Participant Yeah, I think it was mentioned, but it wasn’t like a big part of the conversation. But I guess because I usually only go when I have symptoms, it wasn’t really pushed or anything. Maybe they assumed I wasn’t that interested, or they didn’t want to make me feel uncomfortable by suggesting I come more often. I’ve thought about it a few times, but I just haven’t made it a habit yet.

Interviewer Did you read those leaflets?

Participant I think I gave them a quick look then probably binned them in case anyone else saw. Don't want to read a leaflet about that on the bus you know. I think some of it was quite generic. Stuff I'd heard before. Wear a condom, get tested, that stuff.

Like me, I felt that nervousness the first time I went in. Even though I know that sexual health issues are common right and that everyone should take care of their health, it still seems awkward to talk about it and there might be judgment from family or friends, which I'd be a bit worried about.

It would make it easier to remember, and it would feel more normal to just go in regularly. I think it’s one of those things where, unless something feels wrong, you don’t really think about it. Participant Yeah, I definitely get reminders for other things, like the dentist and the optician. Interviewer I'd like to think more about the reasons you attended the service, and the value it provided. You mentioned getting checked and feeling reassured. It sounds like testing and possibly treatment were your main motivations for attending?

Participant Yeah, that’s it. My reason for going has always been to get checked out when I’m worried about something. The staff were friendly, and they made me feel comfortable even though I was a bit embarrassed to talk about what was going on. On other occasions, I’ve used online booking, which was quicker. That’s definitely made things easier because you can see the available slots and choose a time that suits you without having to call. I think it would be even better if more clinics had that option. So, I’d say it wasn’t too difficult, but there were moments when I wished it was quicker, especially when you’re worried and just want to get it sorted. If they could reduce waiting times a bit or make it easier to book last-minute appointments, that would be a big help.

Interviewer You mentioned nervousness, but also that the staff were friendly. Did you feel welcomed at the service, and like it was aimed you?

Participant Yeah, I did feel welcomed, which was a relief. I was pretty nervous the first time I went—because I didn’t know what to expect, and partly I felt a bit embarrassed about having to talk about my symptoms. But the staff were friendly, and they didn’t make me feel judged or anything. They were calm, professional, and just easy to talk to. That made me relax a bit. As for whether it felt like it was aimed at people like me, I’d say yes and no. I mean, the clinic itself felt quite neutral and welcoming, like anyone could walk in, which is good. But I didn’t really see much that made me feel like it was specifically for people my age or from my background. There were posters and leaflets about general sexual health, contraception, and different infections, but they didn’t really have anything that felt specific to young men, or people from the Asian community, for example. I didn’t feel out of place or anything, but maybe it would’ve been nice to see something that acknowledged people from different backgrounds or had info more tailored to young men. Like, if there were posters or resources that felt more relevant, it might’ve made me feel a bit more connected or maybe I could've learned something useful. I don't know what. But, I’d say the staff made me pretty welcome.

Interviewer And may I ask if you've ever needed treatment?

Participant Yeah, I did need treatment once. It was a while ago, during one of the times I went in because I was having some symptoms that were worrying me. After they did the tests, it turned out I had an infection that needed treating. The staff were really clear about it. They explained what it was, how it could have happened, and what the treatment would involve. I was well embarrassed, but they handled it. Like, it was no big deal and just a normal thing they deal with every day, which made me feel better. They gave me a some antibiotic pills and explained how to take them properly, and they also made sure I understood what I needed to do to avoid passing the infection to anyone else. I was worried it might be complicated, but it was pretty straightforward, and they were open to answering any questions I had. I appreciated how quick it was. I got my results and the treatment on the same day, so I didn’t have to keep stressing about it. It wasn’t a fun experience, obviously, but they made it as easy as possible.

Interviewer And were you asked to return for follow-up testing? Did you attend that appointment?

Participant Yeah, I was asked to come back for a follow-up, they said they need to check if the treatment works. They wanted to make sure that the infection is completely gone. I think they scheduled it for like a month after I finished the pills. I was a bit hesitant at first. I had already gone through the stress of getting tested and then dealing with the treatment, so I wasn’t really happy to go back again. But I understood it was important to make sure, so I did go. That was a lot less nerve-wracking than the first visit. I knew where to go, and the staff, it felt a bit more comfortable. They did the tests and everything came back clear, and I was relieved about it. I’m glad I went back it really put my mind at ease that I was in the clear and didn’t have to worry about it.

Interviewer And during that process, did you feel like you had adequate advice and information about the infection and the treatment?

Participant Yeah I got adequate advice and information. When I first went in and they diagnosed the infection, the staff took some time to explain everything clearly. They talked me through what the infection was, how it might have happened, and what I needed to know about treatment. They gave me some written information, too. That was helpful because I could take it home and read it again later. They encouraged me to ask questions and made it clear that I could call up if I had any concerns after I left the clinic. During the follow-up appointment, they also checked in to see how I was doing and if I had any questions about the treatment or any side effects I might have experienced. It felt reassuring to know they were there to support me through it. Overall, I think they did a great job of providing the right information and making sure I felt informed and confident about what was going on. It made the whole experience a lot less stressful for me.

Interviewer That's great. It sounds like your experience was mostly positive. Did anything about your experience feel like it was less positive, or could have been improved?

Participant Yeah, while my experience was mostly positive, there were definitely a few areas where things could have been better. One thing that stood out was the wait time for an appointment. The first time I went, I had to wait about a week, and when you’re anxious about symptoms, that feels like a long time. It would be great if there were more options for same-day or next-day appointments, especially for people who are worried about something. Another thing was the information about prevention and regular check-ups. While I got good advice when I needed treatment, I think it would have been helpful to have more discussions about how to stay healthy in general—like information on testing, safe sex, and where to find resources that are relevant. And I think the waiting area could be made a bit more comfortable. It's not very inviting. A more relaxed environment might reduce some of the anxiety people feel when they’re waiting.

Interviewer Did you need to share your positive test result with any previous sexual partners? Did you manage to do so, and how did it go?

Participant Yes, I did need to share my positive test result with a previous sexual partner. I knew it was important to let her know so she could get tested and take care of their own health. It was definitely a bit awkward for me to bring it up, but I knew it was the right thing to do. I reached out to her through a message, just to keep it less confrontational. I explained that I had been tested and found out I had an infection, and that it would be good for her to get checked as well. I tried to keep it straightforward and not make it too heavy. She was chill about it and thanked me for letting her know, and said she’d get tested. It felt like a relief to have the conversation, even though I was nervous.

Interviewer Is there anything else you thought was good or bad about your use of the sexual health service?

Participant No, I think that covers it. some of it was good, some things need to be improved. Make it easier to get an appointment, make the information more relevant. Not sure how they can make it less embarrassing, but they should do that.

Interviewer Thanks very much for your time.

Participant Anytime. Hope it goes well.

Interviewer Thank you for agreeing to speak with me today. To start off, have you ever looked for sexual health advice or information? If so, what sources did you find, and which were useful?

Participant Yes, I’ve definitely looked for information before. I'd start with online resources, often websites aimed at gay men's health because I think it's more relevant. I can’t read small text, so I rely on screen magnifiers or high-contrast options, which not every website has. Sometimes I have to trade off information that's really relevant against information that I can read easily enough. It depends, though; if I'm tried after a long day then accessing information is harder, but if I'm relaxed and rested maybe I can work through something less accessible.

Interviewer That sounds frustrating. Did you find any specific websites or resources that were particularly accommodating for your needs?

Participant Forums were quite useful. Often they have buttons for larger text and better contrast built in. One forum had discussions about personal experiences with PrEP, and reading those threads helped me understand it better. I appreciated how real people shared their challenges and successes, which let me know what to expect. But I often had to read through a lot of posts to find the specific information I needed, so it's not always very efficient. The posts often explained how the service really works, though. Like what you need to say to a doctor to get access to PrEP. The NHS website is vaguer about that. I guess they want the doctors to be in charge of what you get, but once I had been reading about PrEP I reallt felt like it was a good thing to do so I wanted to make sure I could get it.

Participant Yes, I’ve been to my local sexual health clinic several times. I first went in to inquire about PrEP, but they told me that regular STI testing like every three months was a requirement to get it.

Participant Mainly, I was looking for PrEP, but I also wanted to get tested for STIs. I knew it was important, being sexually active, and I wanted to make sure I was being responsible of my health, and also being respectful of partners.

Participant My main motivation was to stay safe and responsible. I read how effective PrEP can be, like really reducing HIV, and I really wanted to take control of my sexual health. Participant Trying to book an appointment was annoying. I tried to book online, but their website isn't very accessible. When I finally reached someone, they informed me that I had to wait about two weeks for an appointment. I felt a bit annoyed that it would take so long, but I had read about similar things in the forums so it wasn't a big surprise.

Interviewer That sounds frustrating. How did you feel accessing the service once you got there? Was it comfortable?

Participant Once I arrived, it was a mixed experience. The staff were friendly and were using words that seemed normal, instead of really medical, which made it easier to understand what they meant. Participant Yes, they definitely encouraged me to get tested for STIs, which I appreciated. Interviewer You mentioned being quite ad-hoc, but also that you sometimes need to refill your PrEP supply. Tell me about how that works.

Participant I take PrEP on demand, so it varies how long the supply lasts. When it starts to get low, I need to make another appointment for a refill.

Interviewer And how do you find getting refills?

Participant It's easy enough. You need to plan ahead, though. It can sometimes be a fortnight before you can get an appointment, so I know that when I'm down to less than half a bottle it could be urgent. Participant I think if it's less than a week since the last dose or something, technically you can just take one pill to start the protection as long as you follow up. Sometimes I can't remember how long it's been, or I worry that I've misremembered the rules. I know if I take two pills, wait 2 hours, then I'm protected, and just need to take the two single doses the days following.

Interviewer And do you take those follow doses reliable?

Participant Yes, I think fairly reliably. There are one or two times I have forgotten to take them on time but remembered a few hours later. I think that is OK, but it does worry me a bit that I might completely forget one day and not be protected. Setting an alarm on my phone helps; but you have to remember to do that in advance.

Interviewer That's interesting, thanks for sharing. Have you ever needed treatment for an STI?

Participant Yes, I tested positive for chlamydia once. It was fine, they gave me some antibiotics and then a week or so later it was gone.

Interviewer Can you walk me through that process? What was it like for you?

Participant The initial test was a bit of a surprise. I hadn't experienced it before. When they called me with the results, I felt a bit of a shock. Like, I was worried about people thinking I was there for treatment the first time I went! They reassured me that chlamydia is easily treatable, which calmed me down a bit. Interviewer How did you feel at different points during that process?

Participant Once I got over the initial shock, it was mostly fine. Sitting in the waiting room I felt quite anxious and self-conscious, but once I saw the doctor they were really good and I felt much calmer.

Interviewer Do you think you had adequate information and advice at each stage of that process?

Participant I think so, yes, but I think I could have benefited from more visual aids, like diagrams or charts. Sometimes I felt they assumed I understood everything because I was young, but there were still gaps in my knowledge. I didn't know I should go back for a follow test after the antibiotics, until they explained why. I assumed they just always worked and I could just carry on.

Interviewer Did you need to notify anyone about your positive result? If so, what was that experience like?

Participant Yes, I had to tell my last partner, which was really awkward. I drafted the message several times, worried about how he’d react. But I just sent a straightforward message, and he was very understanding. We discussed it, and he got tested, and didn't make a fuss about it. I guess this stuff happens. Interviewer In general, what do you think has been positive about your engagements with sexual health services?

Participant I think the positive aspect has been the support from the staff. They’ve been mostly understanding and knowledgeable, which I really appreciate. Having access to PrEP and knowing I have options has empowered me to take charge of my sexual health.

Interviewer And what, if anything, has been negative or left room for improvement?

Participant Definitely the accessibility issues. The clinic could do more to ensure their online and physical resources are friendly for people with disabilities. The website navigation is a pain, and I think they could benefit from clearer forms and information when you visit.

Interviewer Thank you for sharing all of this with me.

Participant No problem! I hope it helps.
